.:Neural Networks:.
+neural networks are networks of processors set up to interact with one another. they are computational paradigms that implement a simplified model of the biological brain. the biological brain consists of local neuron assemblages and dendrte connections. neural networks use highly distributed representations and transformations. the use of neural networks allow AI machines to process info in parallel like the brain and perform calculations much faster.
+the perception is a classification of feedfoward neural networks, the nodes, or artificial neurons, in a layer sum their inputs, which may be weighted, and provide a response (output) if a threshold value is reached. there may be a single layer or more of nodes which direct their output to the next layer.
+a node in neural networks is similar to the neuron of the biological system. it receives input from connections and produces output, which depends on the input. a group of these nodes and their connections for other nodes in the subsequent layer. the group of nodes that are at the same hierachical level form a layer. note that info only proceeds forward, and there are generally no loops in the network. groups of nodes are usually divided into three layers in feedforward neural networks: the input layer, the hidden layer, and the output layer. the input layer is analgous to sensory neurons in that it simply gathers data. the hidden layer is similar to interneurons and is responsible for processing the info along with the output layer, which could be compared to motor neurons.
+multi-layer neural networks are usually feed-forward. each node of the preceding layer has a connection with one or more nodes in the subsequent layer. learning techniques, such as back-propagation which requires a loop in the network, are often utilized in multi-layer networks. they work by bringing the network into a steady state when a certain input is present.
+recurrent neural networks differ from feed-forward networks in that data may flow backwards and forwards. subsequent layers may provide output to preceding layers. nodes may even supply input to themselves.
+a neural network learns by training itself on sample patterns and attempts to locate the error between predictions and actual targets. there are algorithms in use for adjusting synaptic weights.
+neural networks provide solutions to very complex and difficult problems. non-linear problems that cannot be solved with standard algorithms may be solved with a neural network.
+training takes just a fraction of the CPU time that error methods take.
+an artificial neural network is a somewhat simplfied model of the biological neuron, which is a brain cell that is assembled together with weighted connections called synapses.
+neural networks can approximate almost any finite dimensional vector function on a set.
+neural networks can extract very complex mathematical functions.
+neural networks can do everything a digital computer can do and more.
+neural networks have the excellent ability to derive meaning from non-linear or imprecise data.

Gingnet

i actually got this idea from a fanfic i was reading a while ago. however, i have absolutely no idea which one it was...i do know that it was on fanfiction.net (megaman obviously). anyways, the concept of Gingnet is this: basically, it is a huge cyber library that has lots of dictionary terms for things in the MMBN universe...like YumCode and BattleChip for example. my version however, will have various computer-related terms in addition to those related to MMBN. this section will one that will always be updated.

  • .:A:.

    • Aqua: an elemental attribute of certain virii and Navis. aqua represents water and ice types. the aqua element is weak to the elec element and is powerful against the heat element.

  • .:B:.

    • BattleChip: a special form of diskette that contains combat data for NetNavis. when loaded into the PET's slot-in drive, the data will be transmitted to the Navi. BattleChips come in a wide variety and are often purchased like trading cards. furthermore, some BattleChips are collected for their rarity.

    Bug: an error in programming that may cause potentially dangerous malfunctions.

  • .:C:.

  • .:D:.

    • Delete: the complete erasing of data. when virii or Navis are damamged enough, their data will be deleted and lost. though Navis can be reprogrammed, their memory data is often lost. the concept of a virus or Navi being deleted is equivalent to the concept of a human being killed.

  • .:E:.

    • Elec: an elemental attribute of certain virii and Navis. elec represents electric types. the elec element is weak to the wood element and is powerful against the aqua element.

    E-Mail: a form of communication exchanged through computers and PETs like letters.

    EXE: a file type representing computer programs. Navis are a form of computer programs, so the suffix "EXE" is part of their name, except when said casually.

  • .:F:.

  • .:G:.

  • .:H:.

    • Heat: an elemental attribute of certain virii and Navis. heat represents fiery types. the heat element is weak to the aqua element and is powerful against the wood element.

  • .:I:.

    • Independent NetNavi: a Navi without a NetOp. independent NetNavis have superior programming and are able to use a variety of powerful attacks without the aid of BattleChips.

  • .:J:.

    • Jack-In: the process of using the PET's wireless transmitter to connect to a device via it's wireless transmitter. when NetOps jack-in and transmit their NetNavi, they often shout this term aloud. moreso, the concept cancelling the wireless connection is referred to as "jack-out".

  • .:K:.

  • .:L:.

    • Log-Out: when a Navi is outside of the PET and is transmitted back to the PET, it logs out of the network. sometimes, when Navis are damaged in certain ways during combat, they are automatically logged out as a sort of "deletion airbag".

  • .:M:.

  • .:N:.

    • 'Net: a cyberworld connected by various networks and computers where Navis can interact in.

    Net Agents: a secret organization of agents that set out to stop NetCrime syndicates. net agents keep their identities a secret, often going out on duty in superhero-like costumes.

    NetBattle: the process of competing in battle against viruses or Navis. NetBattles are usually done for sport, but they are also a neccessity in battling NetCrimes.

    NetBattler: a term referring to someone who NetBattles. almost all NetOps are NetBattlers, so the terms are often interchangable.

    NetCrime: a form of criminal activity performed via the internet using NetNavis and virii.

    NetNavi: short for NetNavigator, NetNavis are a special form of artificial intelligent programming loaded in PETs. Navis are given a unique personality that often reflects upon the NetOperator's personality. Navis perform various tasks on the PET for the NetOperator and can be sent into devices (using the process of jacking-in) to battle computer virii. Navis usually develop a strong friendship with their NetOperators.

    NetOp: short for NetOperator, he/she is the owner and operator of a PET and his/her Navi.

  • .:O:.

  • .:P:.

    • PET: short for either PErsonal Terminal or Personal Exploration Terminal, this handheld device has countless features, ranging from a cell phone to wireless e-mail to even television! PETs are used for personal convenience as well as machinery with the use of jacking-in.

    P.A.: short for Program Advance, this is achieved when three or more BattleChips are loaded into the PET in a certain order. the BattleChips' data combines to form one super-attack. P.A.s are very hard to pull off, for they require that the NetOp and the NetNavi synchronize their breathing.

  • .:Q:.

  • .:R:.

  • .:S:.

    • Slot-In: the process of inserting a BattleChip into the PET's slot-in drive. NetOperators often shout this term (along with the BattleChip's name) when inserting BattleChips.

    Standard Attack: an attack in a Navi's programming that can be performed without the aid of BattleChips.

    StyleChange: a technique only available to those NetNavis that have Chng.BAT installed, StyleChanges allow them to temporarily change their element and weaponry. there are a wide variety of styles.

  • .:T:.

    • Transmission: the sending of a signal, picture, or other info from a transmitter. the act of transmission is used when Navis are sent into devices via jacking-in. the term "transmission" is often shouted aloud by the NetOperator in the process of jacking-in.

  • .:U:.

  • .:V:.

    • Virus: corrupted data in devices that mutate into dangerous virii. once the data has become a virus, it proceeds to disrupt the operating system of the device.

    Virus Busting: the act of using a Navi to delete virii in devices.

  • .:W:.

    • Wood: an elemental attribute of certain virii and Navis. wood represents wooden and plant types. the wood element is weak to the heat element and is powerful against the elec element.

  • .:X:.
